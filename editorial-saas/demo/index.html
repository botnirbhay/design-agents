The premise Finance teams do not need another slide template. They need a reporting system that preserves judgment, source lineage, and the sentence that explains the number.
Product narrative

A close process with a byline, not a scavenger hunt.

Every month-end story has three fragile parts: the metric, the source, and the explanation. Ledger & Line keeps them together while controllers, RevOps, and regional leads edit the same operating narrative.

Drafts read like an executive memo, but behave like software. A margin note can cite the warehouse table, request a regional approval, flag a stale forecast, and export into a board appendix without losing context.
